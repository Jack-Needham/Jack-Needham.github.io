Learnings at 25

Professional

>
  1. Being an entrepreneur is the most sure way to make life-changing amounts of money. It is also the most effective way to have life-changing impact on the world.
  2. Align your business to an industry or technology, one you believe will change the world. This is easier said than done. If you are too late, the winnings have been competed away. It is also easy to think you can revive a dying industry, you are probably wrong.  Picking wrong means you are always swimming upstream.
  3. Most people are mimetic but like to think of themselves as contrarian. Recognise this and spend your time around true contrarian thinkers in industries that you are interested.
  4. Over index on interestingness when starting a business. A few things come downstream of interest. First is good ideas. You are more likely to have a truly novel or contrarian idea in an industry you are naturally rummaging around in. Next is interest is a good forward indicator of grit. Finally, you will be more fulfilled. If you are going to spend a decade building something, do it about a problem you care about.
  5. Interesting people don’t speak the most. They typically exist in non-conformist parts of the internet or academia. They will likely not be at mixers or “events”. If they are, they are usually quiet and not the centre of social groups.
  6. Discipline is a competitive advantage directionally. Be disciplined at tasks that compound competitive advantages and increase momentum.
  7. Timeframes are a competitive advantage. Most people overestimate what happens in a year and underestimate what happens in 10 years. Thinking in longer timeframes changes your decision-making frameworks and allows you to make better long-term bets. Most people aren’t competing in long-term games.
  8. Momentum matters. That doesn’t mean it needs to be easy but you need to believe there is a route out of hardship and towards growth.
  9. Ideas are abundant, not scarce. As a result, don’t be overly protective of ideas. Develop a framework of stress testing ideas to quickly understand their quality.
  10. Action speaks louder than words. Don’t tell people you are great, show them.
  11. Chips on shoulders lead to chips in pockets.
  12. Be competitive. Winning matters and you need to want it. Fuck participation medals. 
  13. Hard work is overrated. Most people aren’t honest with themselves about how productive they are in a day, spending time and energy on non-important tasks. Most people aren’t Elon Musk. Recognise this and design your day accordingly.
  14. Work in sprints. High-intensity work, directed at one thing.
  15. Emotion matters. Human beings feel. Building something that changes an industry or the world requires you to make people feel something.
  16. Failure is overrated. You don’t learn as much as people say you do and typically over-allocate importance to decisions that weren't that bad. Get up, go again, and avoid it at all costs.
  17. Play games where you have a competitive advantage. The bet big.
  18. Losing everything is not as bad as you think it is.
  19. When investing early stage, back people over ideas.
  20. Saying no is usually more important than saying yes. No is a function of honing focus. Focus is very important when building a company.

 

 

 

Personal

  1. Exercise and diet is a way to hone discipline.
  2. Most illness can be cured or prevented by your lifestyle. Most people think they are an improbable victim of disease or illness so don’t act accordingly.
  3. Choose your friends. Most people's friendships come from geographical or situational proximity. This can mean you have low-quality friends. High-quality friendships are a strong forward indicator of a fulfilled and happy life.
  4. It is likely the case that you should do what you are most scared of.
  5. Social media destroys your ability to think clearly and follow a train of thought.
  6. Think in logic, speak in stories.
  7. Develop a willingness or desire to be bad at something publically. People don’t try new things because they don’t have the courage to be bad at something.
  8. Most stress is downstream of an unknown outcome. The second the outcome is defined or observed to be not that bad, stress goes.
  9. Read a lot. More than you think.
